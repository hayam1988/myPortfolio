Stacks

Project Description

Stacks is a data analytics tool created for librarians.

This desktop application Stacks enables librarians to create, view, and update visualizations of library data, in order to better understand their communities needs, and allocate resources more efficiently.

Context

Team Size: 4 Members
Project Length: 10 Weeks
Target User Group: Librarians
Location: University of Washington

Research

As a team, we utilized several research methods to develop an in-depth understanding of our target users.

  • Semi-structured 1:1 interviews
  • Synthesized our findings through Affinity Mapping with sticky notes.
  • Observational analysis, watching five librarians work the “Help Desk” at UW’s Odegaard Undergraduate Library.
  • Create Personas to help us determine user needs.
  • User journey map that details the emotions Librarians face during their day to day. We used these findings to build a better picture of pain points, and better understand our users.

Interviews

We conducted four semi-structured 1:1 interviews with local librarians from the King County Library System (KCLS) and Seattle Public Library.

Take Aways

  • Data Input: Librarians input a wide variety of data into spreadsheets, and there is not a current interface for librarians to interpret and analyze data
  • Event Interfaces: Librarians use multiple interfaces when booking rooms for various events. This causes “large room for human error” when scheduling library events
  • Fear of Relevancy: Librarians indicated worry about decreased library relevancy due to a rise of popularity from other forms of media (movies, streaming, e-books)

Design

Following the research phase, we iterate over the design of Stacks to create a solid foundation for Stack’s design.

  • Design requirements
  • Storyboards
  • Information architecture

    • Design Requirments

    After anaylzing our all ou data collect, we were able to established a list of design requirements. The key findings of our research, allowed us to select the most important user needs, to incoorparate in our final design.

    Users must be able to:

  • View summary data on resource usage
  • Aggregate data freely on different criteria
  • See data over an extensive period of time for their location
  • View and compare visits, checkouts, and event attendance
  • Share created visualizations with fellow librarians
