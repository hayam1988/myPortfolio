 Project Description 

Stacks is a data analytics tool created for librarians.This desktop application enables librarians to create, view and update visualizations of data, in order to better understand the community’s needs and allocate resources more efficiently.

Context

Team Size: 4 Members
Project Length: 10 Weeks
Target User Group: Librarians
Location: University of Washington

The Process

 Research 

As a team, we utilized several research methods to develop an in-depth understanding of our target users.

Overview

  • Semi-structured 1:1 interviews
  • Observational analysis
  • Synthesized our findings through affinity diagramming
  • Create personas
  • User journey map

Interviews

We conducted four semi-structured in-person interviews with local librarians from the King County Library System (KCLS) and Seattle Public Library.

Takeaways

  • Data Input: Librarians input a variety of data into spreadsheets. There is not a current interface for librarians to interpret and analyze data
  • Event Interfaces: Librarians use multiple interfaces when booking rooms for various events. This causes “large room for human error” when scheduling library events
  • Fear of Relevancy: Librarians indicated concern about decreased library relevancy due to a rise of popularity from other forms of media (movies, streaming, e-books)

We also conducted an observational study, watching librarians work at UW’s Odegaard Undergraduate Library.


Takeaways

  • The Information desk has to be able to handle various types of request from visitors (i.e. check-out /check-in various materials, lost and found, general inquiries, etc)
  • Librarians help patrons with questions about where something is
  • Librarians use computers to assist with many inquires

Husky Parking Pal


  Project Description  

Husky Parking Pal is a smartphone GPS application that assists commuters with parking at the University of Washington Bothell. The app supports users with in-advance traffic alerts, parking spot navigation and reservation, digital payment services, and vehicle location recall. Husky Parking Pal enables drivers to have an trouble-free, end-to-end experience while parking at UWB.

Context

Team Size: 6 Members
Project Length: 10 Weeks
Target User Group: Student Commuters
Location: University of Washington - Bothell

The Process


The Hunt Statement & Research Plan

Will explore students enrolled at University of Washington Bothell and examine the difficulties they face when parking on campus. As a result, will uncover human behaviors, norms, and psychological triggers. Will identify how Global Positioning Systems (GPS) will impact the need for a stress-free experience for students when parking on campus.


 Research 

As a team, we utilized several research methods to develop an in-depth understanding of our target users.

Overview

  • Semi-structured in-person interviews
  • Ethnographic study in the UW Bothell parking lot
  • Synthesize our findings
  • Create Personas to determine user needs
  • Questionnaire to reach more target users

Interviews

Each group member, conducted semi-structured 1:1 interviews with UW Bothell students.

Synthesized Findings

  • Students struggle with parking during peak hours
  • Issues with parking has been the cause of being late to class, an on-campus events or a scheduled meetings
  • Each student interviewee could recall being stress, frustrated or angry while looking for on-campus parking
  • Students do anticipate having parking issues and factoring that into their commute time for school
  • During peak busy hours students drive around looking for open spots, otherwise park off campus
  • Students have forgotten where they have parked
  • Students expressed that it would be useful to be pre-notified of a event on-campus impacting parking
  • Full Document

Ethnographic Study

A ethnographic observation was done in the parking lot of UW Bothell.


Takeaways

  • Many students use the kiosk to pay for parking, however, did not wait for receipts if were in a rush
  • Students had to park further away from the UWB buildings which increase walking distance to class
  • The students that were under time pressure seemed to drive faster

Requirements Specifications & Scenarios

The data gathered from the observations, interviews and questionnaires were used to create our functional and non-functional requirements and solidify the direction and design of our Husky Parking Pal.

Some user requirements specification:

  • The system must give live directions to any parking space chosen and/or confirmed by the user
  • Users must be able to reserve a parking spot given the time, date, and preferred lot that the user wants to reserve for
  • The system must prevent users from reserving more than one space with overlapping times
  • The app should not use more battery life than other GPS/navigation apps when running
  • Sending confirmation of payment emails should not take more than 60 seconds
  • The system should handle the user’s information securely to protect their privacy
  • Updates that require the system to be offline will occur at 12:01 am PST

UW Bothell Website Redesign


  Project Description  

The overarching goal of the project is to determine how users interact with the University of Washington Bothell’s homepage, in order to improve usability and user satisfaction and uncover opportunities for a new mobile application. In the end, we will develop and present a set of recommendations for a “client” on how they might improve the effectiveness of UWB homepage website. The final deliverables for the stakeholders are an oral presentation and final report.

Context

Team Size: 7 Members
Project Length: 10 Weeks
Primary User Group: Prospective students & current students
Location: University of Washington-Bothell

The Process


The Hunt Statement & Research Objective

Understand how users interact with the University of Washington Bothell homepage in order to improve usability, (effectiveness/efficiency) and user satisfaction and uncover opportunities for a new mobile application to enhance their lives.

The major research questions addressed are:

  • How to improve uwb.edu site responsiveness?
  • How to increase uwb.edu usability?
  • How to increase focus on the goal of the website?

 Research 

As a team, we utilized several research methods to develop an in-depth understanding of our primary users.

Overview

Usability Test

Each group member conducted usability testing with website visitors, providing feedback on design alternatives. To implement our usability testing the methodology used was A/B testing. We decided A/B testing to be an ideal approach for uncovering which design variants is more functional and usable.

Takeaways for Mobile Navigation Menu

  • In relation to link text size :
    • Participants agreed that an increase in the link’s text size made the menu easier to navigate on a mobile device
    • Participants preferred not having to zoom in to see the links clearer
    • Links are easier to click- on and less likely to click-on the wrong link
  • In relation to structure:
    • Participants preferred the slide out menu vs. a menu bar while using a mobile device
  • In relation to features :
    • Participants did not have a preference that the search bar was included in the menu
  • Misc. results
    • Participant mentioned to help emphasize specific links the color should be different

Husky Football Study


  Project Description  

The focus of this user research study is to better understand fans and their experience while attending a Husky football game on a rainy day. Through various research methods, it is the overreaching goal that solutions are found to improve this experience for Husky fans.

Context

Team Size: Individual
Project Length: 11 Weeks
Primary Inclusion Group: Husky Football Fans
Location: University of Washington

The Process


Design Question

How can we help Husky football fans protect themselves from the rain while at Husky Stadium?

The High Level research questions addressed are:

  • What is the overall experience for fans at a Husky football game on a rainy day?
  • Does the weather discourage fans from having a positive experience while at the game?
  • How do people prepare for Husky games that are rainy?

 Field Study  

The rationale of this research is to better understand the experience of fans while attending a Husky football game on a rainy day. By method of observations, data was gathered to get insight on fan’s behaviors and therefore uncover opportunities to enhance their game day experience.

Overview

  • 3 structured observations
  • 30-minute session each
  • Located at Alaska Airlines Field at Husky Stadium

Methodology

The field studies were conducted by way of three thirty-minute structured observation. This method was chosen because structure observations provides a detailed level of data that comes from disguising and immersing yourself into the environment as one of your participants.


The Data

Each structured observation was steered by predetermined focal points.This structure helped with the scope of data and ensures that the data collected followed a specific criterion.


Focal Points

  • Built environment
  • Information and communication
  • Traffic
  • Possessions
  • Overall experience
  • Tools and technology
